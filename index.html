Malvinas

History is never forgotten

Here's a time line of most important events of the Malvinas war

  • 1520 - Fernando de Magallanes, a Portuguese sailor in the service of Spain, discovers the islands.
  • Jul 9, 1816 - The Tucuman congress makes the final decision and Argentina declares Independence.
  • 1820 - Buenos Aires asserted its rights over the Falkland Islands.
  • 1829 - On June 10, 1829, the government of Buenos Aires appointed the merchant Luis Vernet as political and military commander of the Malvinas. Who since the previous year had their colonization purposes.
  • Dec 31, 1831 - The American corvette Lexington bombed the Argentine installations. They were led by Silas Duncan, a captain in the US military. And after reducing the authorities, looting the dependencies.
  • Jan 3, 1833 - The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland take the Falkland Islands, despite being at peace with the Argentine Confederation, they evict the islands of Argentine soldiers and justify their attack with "Reaffirm authority".
  • Jun 17, 1833 - Manuel Moreno, the Argentine representative, filed a protest in the form of a lengthy document written in both English and French. The "Protest", as it is known, mainly developed the foundations exposed in the decree of appointment of Luis Vernet as governor of the islands of June 10, 1829. The answer came 6 months later and stated that the Spanish Crown had reestablished the English settlement and due to its austerity they belonged to the islands.
  • 1849 - While the second diplomatic protest is ignored by the British they begin colonization in the Falkland Islands.
  • 1884 - Argentina diplomatically claimed the Islands in 1841, 1849, 1884, 1888, 1908, 1927 & 1933, and since 1946 it did so annually before the United Nations. On this date, the official maps began to include the Malvinas Islands as part of the national territory.
  • Apr 2, 1982 - By order of the Argentine military dictatorship, Argentine troops landed on the Malvinas Islands, giving the initial step to the war with the United Kingdom. There were 649 Argentine soldiers killed in action, 255 British soldiers and three inhabitants of the islands. the Argentine troops were mostly conscript soldiers aged 18, 19 and 20.
  • Jun 14, 1982 - Argentina and the United Kingdom, on this date put an end to hostilities. (Mainly Argentina did it because of its situation).
  • Dec 1, 2009 - The Lisbon Treaty gives legal status to the European Union in order to be able to sign international agreements at the community level between the different nations. 27 States of the European Union validate the English usurpation in Malvinas. Argentina rejects this treaty.
  • Dec 26, 2009 - UNASUR supports Argentine claims to the sovereignty of the Malvinas.
  • 2011 - The cause for the sovereignty of Malvinas receives historical support from all the countries of Latin America and the Caribbean represented in CELAC.
  • 2022 - Great Britain continues the depredation of natural resources, the occupation and nuclear militarization of the islands, ignoring the call to dialogue with Argentina established in ONU Resolution 2065.
  • Apr 2, 2022 - Forty years after the malvinas war, argentine ex-combatants once again reaffirm sovereignty over the islands, remembering those who fell in combat and the families who were waiting for their return.
